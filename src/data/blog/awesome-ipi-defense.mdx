There's a repo called [`tldrsec/prompt-injection-defenses`](https://github.com/tldrsec/prompt-injection-defenses) that used to be the canonical place to track work on prompt injection defenses. It was well-curated and widely linked. The problem: it hasn't been updated in over a year.

Meanwhile, the research community did not get the memo. New defense papers keep landing on arxiv at a steady clip — detection methods, isolation architectures, finetuning approaches, formal frameworks — and none of them are making it into that list.

So I built a replacement: [`Rem1L/awesome-ipi-defense`](https://github.com/Rem1L/awesome-ipi-defense).

The goal is simple: keep a living, reasonably comprehensive index of work on indirect prompt injection (IPI) defenses. Papers, blog posts, benchmarks, anything that seriously engages with the question of *how do you stop an LLM agent from being hijacked by malicious content in its context*.

It's early. The initial version is seeded from what I could find across arxiv, the old tldrsec list, and some papers I've read over the past year. There will be gaps. If you know of something missing, PRs are open.

One thing I want to avoid is the same fate as its predecessor: going dark after the initial burst of enthusiasm. I'll do my best to keep it maintained, but frankly the best insurance against that is community involvement. If the list is useful to you, the healthiest thing you can do is star it, watch it, and send corrections when you find them.

Prompt injection defense is still an open problem. The least we can do is keep a decent map of where things stand.
</En>
